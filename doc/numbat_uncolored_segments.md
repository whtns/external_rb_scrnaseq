# Why Segments in `bulk_clones` / `bulk_subtrees` Are Uncolored Despite Visible AF and Expression Deflection

Numbat's bulk plots (`bulk_clones`, `bulk_subtrees`) show the raw allele frequency (BAF) and
expression signal, but segment coloring reflects the posterior CNV call — these are not the same
thing. A segment can display clear deflection in both modalities while remaining uncolored for
several reasons.

## `p_cnv` vs `p_min`: an important distinction

**`p_cnv` is a computed output, not a parameter you set.** Numbat calculates it per cell × segment
during the run — it is the posterior probability that a CNV exists at that locus in that cell.

**`p_min` is the plotting threshold** you control. It determines which segments get colored in
our custom heatmaps (`plot_numbat` → `plot_phylo_heatmap`). Decreasing `p_min` from 0.9 to e.g.
0.5 will color segments with weaker, more ambiguous evidence. The pipeline already has this as
`fig_s13` (`p_min = 0.5`).

**`bulk_clones` and `bulk_subtrees` are different.** These are PNGs generated by numbat during
the run itself — our `p_min` parameter has no effect on them. Their coloring is governed by
numbat's internal confidence scoring at run time. To change what gets colored there you would need
to re-run numbat with different parameters (e.g. `alpha` — the prior probability of CNV — or
`min_cells`).

| Plot | What controls coloring | Changeable without re-run? |
|---|---|---|
| Our heatmaps (`fig_s03a_*`) | `p_min` in `plot_numbat` | Yes — decrease `p_min` |
| `bulk_clones` / `bulk_subtrees` | Numbat run parameters (`alpha`, `min_cells`) | No — must re-run numbat |

If segments are consistently uncolored in `bulk_clones` but show clear signal, the most likely fix
is re-running numbat with a higher `alpha` (more permissive CNV prior).

## Reasons a segment may show signal but not be colored

### 1. `p_cnv` Threshold Not Met (Most Common)

Coloring requires `p_cnv` to exceed `p_min` (default 0.9 in this pipeline). The HMM posterior
integrates BAF and expression signal across all cells and imposes a strong prior. A segment
present in a minority subclone will show a visible but diluted bulk deflection while the per-cell
`p_cnv` distribution may never concentrate above threshold.

### 2. Subclonal Dilution

`bulk_clones` aggregates signal across all cells in a clone. If a CNV exists in only a fraction of
one clone, the bulk AF deflection is real but diluted. At the single-cell level, most cells in
that clone may have ambiguous `p_cnv` (0.3–0.7), landing in the midline range and preventing a
confident call. This is the same dynamic addressed by the `filter_midline` parameter in
`make_numbat_heatmaps` and `make_rb_scna_ideograms`.

### 3. Non-Corroborated Unimodal Signal

Numbat calls CNVs by jointly integrating BAF *and* expression. If only one modality is deflected:

- Expression change without allele imbalance → could reflect hypoxia, cell cycle, or technical
  variation rather than copy number; posterior stays low.
- Allele imbalance without expression change → consistent with copy-neutral LOH; may be assigned
  to `p_loh` rather than `p_cnv`.

The combined posterior must be convincing across both modalities for a colored call.

### 4. Copy-Neutral LOH

LOH without a copy number change produces BAF deflection with approximately normal expression.
Numbat tracks this as a separate state (`loh`). Depending on which posterior wins, the segment may
appear with a distinct LOH color, or no color if neither state clears threshold.

### 5. HMM Segmentation Boundaries

The HMM operates on genomic segments, not individual genes. A segment with borderline evidence
may be merged with neighboring diploid segments during Viterbi decoding, causing the signal to
appear in the bulk plot but not survive as a discrete called segment.

## Summary

| Observation | Likely cause |
|---|---|
| Deflected BAF + expression, `p_cnv` < 0.9 | Subclonal dilution; threshold not met |
| BAF deflection, no expression change | Copy-neutral LOH; state assigned to `loh` |
| Expression change, no BAF deflection | Non-CNV source (hypoxia, cell cycle) |
| Signal in bulk but absent from heatmap | Midline ambiguity; `filter_midline` removed segment |
| Colored in our heatmap but not in `bulk_clones` PNG | `p_min` differs from numbat's internal threshold |

The bulk plots are diagnostic — they show the raw evidence. The coloring reflects confidence after
full Bayesian integration across all cells and both modalities.
